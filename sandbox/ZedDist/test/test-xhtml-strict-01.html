Introduction to the Z39.86-2011 Strict XHTML profile

Unique Traits

Sections and headings

To mimic the cozy warm feeling of the DTBook level element, this profile elevates the section and hx elements to a special status:

  • the section element is used solely to encapsulate major structural portions of the document (i.e. section alone constructs the document outline)
  • a section can contain 0 or 1 h1-h6. Sections without a heading are equally structurally significant as those with a heading
  • the body element has the content model of a top-level section, and is thus the only element that can contain 0 or 1 h1
  • The use of h1-h6 must follow proper nesting (e.g. section children of body can only use h2 etc)

The div element is considered a structurally less significant, and is used for grouping in contexts (typically Flow) where sections are not allowed.

Flow content

As compared to loose (X)HTML, Flow content is of an either-or nature. This means that the children of list items and table cells for example, must be either inline or block, but not both at the same time at time.

Empty elements

Certain elements, such as p, are not allowed to be empty. A p with only whitespace is also considered invalid. (TODO -- this may be a req on loose as well).
